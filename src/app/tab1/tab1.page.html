Oulu is Northern Finland’s largest and oldest city, located in the Gulf of Bothnia in the Oulujoki River delta. In the 19th century, Oulu was the town was Finland’s leading exporter of tar. Today, the city is home to more than 200,000 locals, and is a favourite destination for thousands of domestic and international leisure and business travellers.


Oulu consists of the cultural life of a modern business and tourism city. The uniqueness of the city is based on the fact that the hustle and bustle of the city centre areas make it easy to deviate to the peace and the greenery of the park, the sandy beach or the frozen sea.
